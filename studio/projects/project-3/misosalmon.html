Miso Salmon

Miso salmon with broccoli and rice

High protein, Low Fat

This spicy miso‐glazed airfried salmon can be made in under 30 minutes and pairs perfectly with broccoli and fluffy rice. It’s salty, a little sweet, and packed with protein to keep you fueled until your next meal.

Ingredients

2 filets of Salmon
2–4 cloves of Garlic
2 tbsp Miso Paste
1 tbsp Gochujang (Optional)
½ tbsp Soy Sauce
½ tbsp Mirin
½ tbsp Brown Sugar
½ tbsp Sesame Oil
½ tbsp Kewpie Mayo (Optional)
1 head Broccoli
1 cup Rice

Estimated cost of produce: $10–15

Instructions
  1. Wash and cook one cup of rice in rice cooker or pot (Ratio of rice to water should be 1:2).
  2. Combine 3/4 of the minced garlic with all the sauces into a medium sized mixing bowl.
  3. Wash and pat dry the salmon with a paper towel.
  4. Slice the salmon up into bite size pieces (roughly 2 inch by 2 inch).
  5. Mix the sliced salmon into the garlic and sauce mixture, and set in fridge to let marinate for 10-20 mins (can marinate for up to 24 hours).
  6. Meanwhile, wash and prep the broccoli by cutting it into florets.
  7. Place broccoli into a pot of salted boiling water for 2-4 minutes (until tender).
  8. Place the marinated salmon pieces into an airfryer lined with parchment paper, airfry for 10 minutes at 300 degrees fahrenheit (165 celcius).
  9. Strain water from broccoli and add in the remaining minced garlic (add salt to taste).
  10. Once rice is cooked, assemble it onto a plate with the salmon and broccoli (Optional: top the rice with furikake).
